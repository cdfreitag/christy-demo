Upper Peninsula Welcomes Ice Climbers at 2025 Michigan Ice Fest

By Christiana Freitag

On the frozen shorelines of Lake Superior, winter thrill-seekers will be welcomed with 150 ice climbs and a festival of celebration at the 2025 Michigan Ice Fest.

For over 30 years, Munising, Michigan has hosted outdoor enthusiasts at the ice climbing fest, the second-largest in the world. The festival brings in over around 1,000 attendees each year since the festival began back in 1990.

From February 12 - 16, the festival will host speakers, ice climbing clinics and celebrations for attendees.

Source: Michigan Ice Fest

The Michigan Ice Fest is among the top ice climbing festivals in the world. Beyond the world-class climbers that attend and speak at the festival, Michigan Ice Climbing Festival organizer, Bill Thompson credits the popularity of the festival to its unique location on Lake Superior.

"One of the unique things about ice climbing in Michigan is we have something nobody else has, and that’s Lake Superior," Thompson said. "Climbing over open water is something you can’t do anywhere else in the world. We have miles and miles of ice. Lots of ice, Lake Superior – it’s the place to climb."
